Small Molecule - Therapeutic Drug Monitoring

I have a patent interest in small molecular interactions with proteins. This started with my work in clinical research laboratory work at Consolidate Laboratory Services (CLS). Uunder the tuteladge of Dr. Sonny Gunawan, I leaned many aspects of pharmacology (e.g., pharmicokientics, bioavailabity, drug-drug interaction) and practical chemistry (e.g., liquid-liquid extraction, TLC and HPLC). In his Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM), Dr. Gunawan showed me how to develop CLIA certified assays, which analyzed the serum level of antiretroviral drugs using advanced LC-MS/MS protocols. By the end of my tenure at CLS, I was developing assays that could simutaneously analyze up to three antiretrovirals at a time to allow clinicians to maintain proper serum therapeutic levels of HAART regiments.


Enzymes - Obesity

Many of the encoded proteins in our gene discovery methods have putative enzymatic which is particullarly true of one of our most exciting new genes, LDAH. This putative cholesteryl esterase is located on lipid droplets and may act in partnership with the ACAT gene family to maintain proper intracellular cholesterol and lipid levels. We are working closed with Dr. Alex Banks and other groups to examine the function of LDAH using both standard methods (e.g., colorimetric enzymatic assays) and more advanced techniques (e.g., activity based protein profiles). Given that LDAH has been associated with elevated BMI and lipid dysregulation, and the fact that we see elevated body mass in our KO mouse model, understanding the cholesteryl esterase function of this enzyme could hold a key to therapeutic intervention of the growing obesity epidemic.
