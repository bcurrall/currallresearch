Biochemistry

Protein-Protein Interactions

During my graduate research with Dr. Richard Hallworth, I began intensity of the structure-function relationship of the prestin, an exquisite protein that functions as an piezoelectrode that drives the somatic motility of the inner ear outer hair cells. We examined the quaternary structure of prestin by looking at the fluorescent lifetimes of fluorescently labeled subunits in a process known as FLIM. The lifetime of these fluorophores would change if near another fluorophore (known as FRET). This work, along with bioinformatics and electrophysiology analysis of prestin, formed the heart of my doctoral dissertaion and led to further work on other protein-protein interactions with cochlin and GLUT5.


Electrophysiology

My graduate studies started with Dr. David He exploring the elecrophysiology properties of outer hair cells. This was the first time I saw hair cells "dance" and have been intrigued by this phenomena ever since. Under David, I studied the effects that ototoxic drugs have on the electromotility of hair cells and analyzed the current signatures of prestin.
